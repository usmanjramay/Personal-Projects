## ARCHITECT'S KEY CLAIMS

### Claim 1: Work Was Authorized

> "Despite our amicable proposals aimed at reaching a common agreement regarding the additional services developed throughout your project to transform an existing building into a building comprising four apartments during the year 2025, no agreement could be reached."

**Analysis:** Architect admits "no agreement could be reached" — meaning he performed work without finalized agreement.

### Claim 2: You Were "Fully Informed"

> "You have been fully informed of the detailed content of this study and have confirmed its direction in your email, in which you specify that, in the event that the construction of five apartments would not be possible, a project of four apartments should be developed."

**Evidence Cited:** Email mentioning "If 5 apartments are not possible with that, then we should stick to 4"

**Analysis:** Architect uses a conditional statement ("if 5 not possible, then 4") as authorization for full 4-apartment plan development. This is a misrepresentation — a conditional statement about feasibility is NOT authorization for work and billing.

### Claim 3: Initial Offer Didn't Cover This Work

> "Our initial basic offer only covered the regulatory feasibility analysis for 3 or 5 apartments and did not include the complete development of a project configured for a number of apartments whose actual potential turned out to be four. It turned out that the regulations do indeed allow for a project of four apartments."

**Analysis:** This actually **supports your defense** — architect admits original contract didn't cover 4-apartment plan development. This proves it should have been a separate authorized contract.

### Claim 4: Mutual Agreement Strategy

> "It was mutually agreed that we would initially submit a permit application for a single-family home that nevertheless meets all the requirements of a four-apartment building, with the application for a four-apartment building to be submitted later."

**Analysis:** Architect claims "mutually agreed" but provides no written evidence of this agreement. WhatsApp messages show no such agreement on your part.
