## 1. IS IT SAFE TO MENTION COMPLAINTS IN THE LETTER?

**Short answer: Yes, but phrasing matters critically.**

Under Luxembourg/French law, threatening to exercise a legal right (filing with a regulator) is **NOT blackmail (chantage)**. Confirmed by Cour de cassation: *"la menace d'exercer une voie de droit ne constituait pas un chantage."*

**One requirement:** The complaint must be connected to the same dispute. Ours clearly is.

### Phrasing Risk Table

| Phrasing | Risk | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| "If you don't withdraw, we WILL file complaints" | HIGH | Reads as conditional deal / quid pro quo — chantage territory |
| "We reserve the right to file complaints" | LOW | Standard legal reservation — accepted practice |
| "Your conduct appears to engage the provisions of [law]..." | NONE | States a legal observation, not a threat — fully defensible |

**Use the third option.** Don't say you will or won't file. Name the laws. Let the laws speak.

## 2. CORRECT LETTER FORMAT: MISE EN DEMEURE

This letter must open as a formal **mise en demeure** (Articles 1344–1344-2 Code civil).

Opening line should include:
> *"Par la présente lettre recommandée avec accusé de réception, nous vous mettons en demeure de..."*

This is legally significant: it starts the clock on late-payment interest, is a prerequisite for many court actions, and establishes the formal record date.

## 3. OAI — OFFICIAL FRENCH TERMS

**Full name:** Ordre des Architectes et des Ingénieurs-Conseils (OAI)

**Governing law:** Loi du 13 décembre 1989 portant organisation des professions d'architecte et d'ingénieur-conseil

**Ethics regulation:** Règlement grand-ducal du 17 juin 1992 déterminant la déontologie des architectes et des ingénieurs-conseils

**Disciplinary body:** Conseil de discipline (Art. 22, Loi du 13 décembre 1989)

**First-stage review body:** Commission OAI « Déontologie »

**Key French terms:**

| What we're alleging | French term |
|---|---|
| Ethics violation | Manquement à la déontologie |
| Violation of professional obligations | Manquement aux obligations professionnelles |
| Acting beyond authorized scope | Dépassement de mission / actes hors mission |
| Unauthorized representation to authorities | Représentation sans pouvoir / acts beyond mandate (Art. 1998 Code civil) |
| Disciplinary complaint | Plainte auprès de la Commission Déontologie de l'OAI |
| Referral to disciplinary council | Renvoi devant le Conseil de discipline |

**Safe reference language for letter:**
> *"Nous attirons votre attention sur le fait que votre conduite semble constituer des manquements à la déontologie au sens du Règlement grand-ducal du 17 juin 1992 déterminant la déontologie des architectes et des ingénieurs-conseils, susceptibles de tomber sous la compétence du Conseil de discipline de l'OAI en vertu de l'article 22 de la loi du 13 décembre 1989."*

## 4. DPC — OFFICIAL FRENCH TERMS

**Full name:** Direction de la protection des consommateurs (**DPC** — not "DCP")

**Parent ministry (2026):** Ministère de l'Agriculture, de l'Alimentation et de la Viticulture. The DPC is a *Direction* (directorate) under this ministry — **not** a standalone Ministère de la Protection des Consommateurs, despite what the legacy web domain (`mpc.gouvernement.lu`) might suggest. The old "MPC" ministry was absorbed; the Direction kept the URL for continuity.

**Website:** https://mpc.gouvernement.lu/fr.html

**Official self-description:** *"La Direction de la protection des consommateurs auprès du Ministère de l'Agriculture, de l'Alimentation et de la Viticulture au Luxembourg s'engage pleinement à assurer la protection et les droits des consommateurs."*

**Governing law:** Code de la consommation luxembourgeois

**Key French terms and article numbers:**

| What we're alleging | French term | Article |
|---|---|---|
| Unfair commercial practices (general prohibition) | Pratiques commerciales déloyales | Art. L. 122-1 |
| Misleading practices — actions (false advice re: plan modifiability; false timeline) | Pratiques commerciales trompeuses (actions) | Art. L. 122-2 |
| Misleading practices — omissions (missing cost estimate) | Pratiques commerciales trompeuses (omissions) | Art. L. 122-3 |
| Aggressive practices — general definition | Pratiques commerciales agressives | Art. L. 122-5 |
| Aggressive practices — assessment factors (9-day deadline, obstacle to terminating) | Pratiques commerciales agressives (éléments d'appréciation) | Art. L. 122-6, notamment (d) et (e) |
| Aggressive practices — per se blacklist (demanding payment for unrequested services) | Pratiques commerciales agressives (liste noire) | Art. L. 122-7, notamment point 6 |
| Failure of professional diligence | Manquement à la diligence professionnelle | Art. L. 122-1(1) (la définition est à l'art. L. 121-2, point 6) |
| Enforcement / cessation order | Action en cessation | Art. L. 320-2 *(to verify — not re-confirmed in 2026-04-16 review)* |
| Fine range | €251 – €120,000 (up to 4% turnover under Omnibus Directive) | Art. L. 320-2 *(to verify)* |

**Safe reference language for letter:**
> *"De même, votre conduite semble susceptible de constituer des pratiques commerciales déloyales au sens de l'article L. 122-1 du Code de la consommation luxembourgeois, et notamment des pratiques commerciales trompeuses (articles L. 122-2 et L. 122-3) et des pratiques commerciales agressives (articles L. 122-5 à L. 122-7, en particulier l'article L. 122-7, point 6, qui répute déloyale en toutes circonstances toute exigence de paiement pour des services que le consommateur n'a pas demandés), relevant du contrôle de la Direction de la protection des consommateurs (DPC)."*

## 5. RESERVATION CLAUSE (END OF COMPLAINTS SECTION)

Always close the section with:
> *"Nous nous réservons tous droits et actions à cet égard, sans que la présente lettre ne constitue une renonciation à quelque droit que ce soit."*

("We reserve all rights and actions in this regard, without this letter constituting a waiver of any right whatsoever.")

## 6. SUMMARY: WHAT TO SAY vs. WHAT TO AVOID

| DO say | DON'T say |
|---|---|
| "Your conduct appears to engage Art. L.122-2 Code de la consommation..." | "If you don't withdraw, we will file with the DPC" |
| "...susceptibles de constituer un manquement à la déontologie..." | "We will report you to the OAI" |
| "Nous nous réservons tous droits et actions" | "Unless you comply by [date], we will file complaints" |
| Name the laws and let them speak | Create any conditional structure (if X then Y) around the complaints |

## 8. COLLABORATION / EXCLUSIVITY CLAUSES IN ARCHITECT CONTRACTS

**Context:** Our signed Nov 28, 2024 contract contains a clause binding us to continue collaborating with the same architectural firm for the various options analyzed, without defined price, scope, timeline, or duration for those future options. The architect's 07.04.2026 demand letter invokes this clause as partial support for his €33,930 claim.

**Strategic choice made for v3 demand response (2026-04-16):** NOT engaged in the body of our response. Reasons:
1. The clause is not load-bearing for his claim — his €33,930 demand rests on alleged authorization via our Jan 12, 2025 email, not on this clause.
2. Engaging with it tacitly concedes the clause has some binding effect. Silence avoids that concession.
3. The reservation-of-rights paragraph preserves every defense, so silence ≠ waiver.

**Arguments to deploy if/when the clause is raised again** (future correspondence, small-claims case, court defense):

| Angle | Authority | Argument |
|---|---|---|
| Indeterminate object | Art. 1129 Code civil (Luxembourg kept the pre-2016 Napoleonic text) | Contract object must be determined or at least determinable. A commitment to a future project at unknown price, unknown scope, and unknown timeline is an agreement to agree — not a binding commitment. |
| Abusive clause in B2C contract | Art. L.211-2 et seq. Code de la consommation (implementing Directive 93/13/EEC on unfair terms) | Creates significant imbalance to the detriment of the consumer: consumer is locked in; architect owes nothing reciprocal (no price cap, no duty to provide service on demand, no defined timeline). Textbook unfair-term pattern. |
| Perpetual engagement | Luxembourg/French civil-law principle (*prohibition des engagements perpétuels*) | Indefinite personal commitments are presumptively unenforceable. This clause has no time limit. |
| Deontology | Art. 9 Règlement grand-ducal du 17 juin 1992 (OAI) | Any significant modification of the programme must be the subject of an *additional written convention stating the financial impact*. A generic upstream collaboration clause cannot substitute for Art. 9's requirement. |
| Good faith | Art. 1134 Code civil | Contracts must be performed in good faith. Invoking this clause as a basis to bill for unauthorized work is inconsistent with good faith. |

**To verify before court filing:** Luxembourg case law specifically addressing *"clauses de collaboration exclusive"* or *"clauses d'exclusivité"* in architect-consumer (B2C) contracts. Worth a targeted search if the clause becomes a live issue.

**Practical takeaway:** Even if enforceable, the clause binds us (at most) to use the same architect *if* we choose to pursue an analyzed option. It does not give the architect the right to bill for work we never authorized. That is the most important limiting principle — and is the last line we would retreat to if all the invalidity arguments failed.
