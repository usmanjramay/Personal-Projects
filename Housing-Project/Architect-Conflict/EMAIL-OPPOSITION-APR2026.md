## Context

The court clerk confirmed by phone that a short written opposition (a few lines) is sufficient at the filing stage. Luxembourg law (Art. 131 NCPC) requires only *"l'indication sommaire des motifs"* — a summary indication of the grounds. The merits are developed later at the hearing, if the creditor pursues the claim.

The opposition invokes two doctrines as the grounds for refusing payment:

1. **Exception d'inexécution** (defensive; grounded in Art. 1134 Code civil and settled Luxembourg jurisprudence) — immediate basis for withholding the third installment while the Architect remains in non-performance.
2. **Article 1184 Code civil** (threatened remedy) — judicial résolution of the contract, with retroactive effect (*ex tunc*) entailing restitution of sums already paid (€6,581.25) and damages.

A separate **mise en demeure** to the Architect should be sent by registered letter in the days/weeks following this opposition, to strengthen both doctrines ahead of any hearing. See @COURT-OPPOSITION-DRAFT-APR2026.md (repurposed as the mise en demeure working draft).
