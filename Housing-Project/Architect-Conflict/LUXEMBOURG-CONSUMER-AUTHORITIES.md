# Luxembourg Consumer Protection & Architect Complaint Framework

**Last Updated:** April 2026  
**Research Scope:** Consumer rights, complaint procedures, and architect-specific disciplinary processes in Luxembourg

---

## Part 1: Consumer Protection Authorities & Rights

### Key Consumer Rights in Luxembourg

Consumers have the right to:
- **Safety** — products and services must be safe
- **Fair treatment** — transparent pricing and honest business practices
- **Transparency** — clear contract terms and information disclosure
- **Redress** — ability to seek compensation and resolution
- **Withdrawal** — 14-day cancellation period on certain distance/off-premises contracts

**Source:** [Directorate for Consumer Protection](https://mpc.gouvernement.lu/en/le-ministere/protjur.html)

### 2. **Service National du Médiateur de la Consommation** — *Out-of-Court Dispute Resolution*

**Role:** Free, neutral, impartial mediator for consumer-professional disputes  
**Government Agency:** Ministry of Economy  
**Scope:** Any consumer dispute from a service contract

**Prerequisites to File:**
- You must have already attempted to resolve the dispute directly with the professional

**How to File:**

| Method | Contact Info |
|--------|--------------|
| **Post** | 6, rue du Palais de Justice, 1841 Luxembourg |
| **Email** | infomediateurconsommation.lu |
| **Phone** | +352 46 13 11 |
| **Fax** | +352 46 36 03 |
| **Online Form** | Available on www.mediateurconsommation.lu |

**What to Include:**
- Summary of the situation (can be in Luxembourgish, French, English, or German)
- Your expectations/requested resolution
- All relevant documents and correspondence

**Timeline:**
- Resolution process: max 90 days from when mediator receives all information (extendable for complex cases)
- Process is **completely free**
- All communications are **confidential**
- Either party can withdraw at any time

**Mediator's Role:**
- Acts as impartial intermediary to facilitate dialogue
- Can propose a solution (non-binding — parties free to accept or reject)

**Source:** [Médiateur de la Consommation](https://www.mediateurconsommation.lu/) | [Guichet.lu - Consumer Mediator](https://guichet.public.lu/fr/citoyens/justice/voies-recours-reglement-litiges/resolution-litige-amiable/mediateur-consommation.html)

## Part 3: Architect-Specific Complaint & Disciplinary Framework

### **OAI (Ordre des Architectes et des Ingénieurs-Conseils)**

**What It Is:** Luxembourg's professional order/regulatory body for architects and consulting engineers  
**Legal Status:** Professional organization with disciplinary authority

#### Grounds for Disciplinary Action

The OAI can investigate and discipline members for:
- Violations of legal and regulatory prescriptions concerning professional practice
- **Acts contrary to professional delicacy and dignity** ← *Key for your case*
- **Violations of honor and probity** ← *Relevant to unprofessional conduct*
- Professional ethics breaches

#### Complaint Procedure

**Step 1: Initial Complaint Submission**
- File complaint to OAI with supporting documents and evidence
- Must provide clear description of the violation

**Step 2: Commission Review & Dialogue**
- OAI Commission reviews the complaint
- Initiates dialogue with the architect
- Exchange of position statements between parties
- **Amicable settlement often achieved at this stage**

**Step 3: Ethics Investigation (if needed)**
- If no settlement, Commission examines file for ethics violations
- Determines if professional rules were breached

**Step 4: Discipline Council (if case proceeds)**
- Case may be referred to Discipline Council for formal proceedings
- Composed of:
  - President: President of Luxembourg District Court (or designate)
  - Members: 2 representatives from OAI (one architect, one engineer)

**Historical Note:** Over 30+ years, only ~12 disciplinary cases total; last formal Discipline Council procedure was June 2010. **This suggests complaints are usually resolved at Commission stage.**

#### Common Violations Relevant to Your Case

Based on professional ethics standards:
1. **Scope of work violation** — Performing work beyond contract without authorization
2. **Unjust enrichment** — Billing for unsolicited work
3. **Lack of transparency** — Failing to inform client about extra work before proceeding
4. **Unprofessional conduct** — Threatening/coercive payment tactics
5. **Breach of good faith** — Misrepresenting work completed vs. contracted

#### How to File a Complaint with OAI

**Contact Information:**
- Website: www.oai.lu
- For client dispute resolution: oai.lu/pour-maitres-d-ouvrage/resoudre-un-litige/

**What to Include:**
- Architect's name and professional details
- Clear description of professional misconduct
- Specific contract terms that were violated
- Timeline of events
- All supporting documents (contract, emails, invoices, photos, etc.)
- Your position statement

**Source:** [OAI Dispute Resolution for Clients](https://www.oai.lu/fr/15/accueil/pour-maitres-d-ouvrage/resoudre-un-litige/?CC=OK) | [OAI Legislation & Regulation](https://www.oai.lu/files/Publications/Legislation/legisoai6.pdf)

## Part 4: Construction/Renovation-Specific Consumer Rights

### Special Provisions for Construction & Renovation Services

For construction and renovation defects, Luxembourg law provides:

**Who Can Be Held Liable:**
- The seller or builder
- The architect
- Subcontractors
- Project managers

**Remedies Available:**
- Expert inspection to document defects
- Determination of repair costs
- Valuation of property loss
- Cost recovery through court action

**Source:** [Guichet.lu - Construction Defects & Reparation](https://guichet.public.lu/en/citoyens/logement/construction-renovation-transformation/travaux/vice-construction.html)

## Part 5: Unfair Commercial Practices

### Reporting Unfair Practices (Relevant to Your Case)

**Types of Unfair Practices:**
- Misleading claims about work completed
- Coercive billing tactics
- Threatening language ("pay or face court")
- Claiming work was done without authorization

**How to Report:**
- File with **Directorate for Consumer Protection** (see Part 2)
- Report to **Service du Médiateur** as part of dispute resolution

**Source:** [Guichet.lu - Reporting Unfair Practices](https://guichet.public.lu/en/citoyens/citoyennete/protection-consommateur/pratiques-deloyales/denonciation-pratique-deloyale.html)

## Part 6: Collective Redress (New 2025)

**Recent Development:** Luxembourg implemented collective redress in consumer law (Bill No. 7650, transposing EU Directive 2020/1828).

**Relevance:** If multiple customers have disputes with the same architect over similar misconduct, collective redress may be available.

**Source:** [Luxembourg Collective Redress Implementation](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=ad542b9f-11e8-478e-a4c5-8e8e8a55feb4)

## Summary: Recommended Complaint Strategy for Your Case

### **Path 1: Immediate Action (Recommended - Parallel Track)**

1. **File with Mediator of Consumption** (Free, Fast, Confidential)
   - Fast resolution (90 days max)
   - No court needed
   - Can happen while responding to court case

2. **File with Directorate for Consumer Protection** (Enforcement)
   - Documents unfair practices
   - Creates official record
   - May trigger investigation

3. **File Complaint with OAI** (Professional Discipline)
   - Targets professional misconduct specifically
   - References contract violations, scope creep, unprofessional conduct
   - Creates disciplinary record

### **Path 2: If Architect Escalates to Court**

- You have formal records from all three complaint processes
- OAI and DCP complaints create independent evidence of misconduct
- Mediator records provide documented good-faith attempt to resolve

### **Key Evidence to Gather Now**

- [ ] Original contract (with scope clearly defined)
- [ ] All email communications with architect
- [ ] Initial invoice for €8,000 (court case)
- [ ] €33,000 demand letter
- [ ] Any communications about project delays
- [ ] Proof of incomplete work (missing interior plans)
- [ ] Court summons regarding €8,000
- [ ] Any threatening language from architect
