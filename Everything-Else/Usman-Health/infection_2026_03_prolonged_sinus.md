## Analysis: Sinus Anatomy & Drainage Pattern

### Why Nothing Comes Out (Front Nostril)
The four sinus pairs drain to different locations:
- **Maxillary sinuses** (largest, under cheekbones) → drain via middle meatus (inward/downward)
- **Ethmoid sinuses** (deep, between eyes) → drain via middle meatus (front) or superior meatus (back)
- **Sphenoid sinuses** (deep in skull) → drain into sphenoethmoidal recess (upper/posterior)
- **Frontal sinuses** (forehead) → drain via middle meatus (downward)

**Key finding:** Most sinus drainage is *inward and downward*, NOT forward out the nose. If saline irrigation produces no front-nostril output, inflammation is likely in:
1. **Maxillary sinuses** (causes side pressure, post-nasal drip down throat) — MOST LIKELY based on symptoms
2. **Deep ethmoid sinuses** (between eyes, lateral pressure)

### Corrected Irrigation Technique
- Direct water *down throat* instead of front nostril
- Lean forward 90 degrees, head rotated so one ear points down
- Open mouth, let water drain posteriorly
- This reaches deeper sinus cavities

### Key Insight: Eustachian Tube Connection & Drainage Verification (2026-04-11)
**Symptom:** Ear pressure + popping sensation when yawning or stretching jaw; audible pop during irrigation.

**Interpretation:** 
- Sinus inflammation backs up into middle ear via Eustachian tube
- Popping = pressure equalization, indicating deep sinus drainage pathways are responding to irrigation
- Water cannot exit front nostril because primary inflammation is in maxillary/ethmoid sinuses, which drain *inward/downward* toward throat and ear, not forward

**Expected drainage pattern after irrigation:**
1. Day 1: Loosened mucus → increased post-nasal drip (not dramatic water flow)
2. Day 2-3: Lysomucil thins secretions further; drip transitions from "blocked sinus" to "back of throat" drainage sensation
3. Day 3-4: Continued thinning and posterior drainage; ear pressure may fluctuate as Eustachian tube clears

**Success metric:** Not "water comes out front nostril," but "increased post-nasal drip + ear popping + gradual transition to throat-based drainage."
